When to Consider

Signs It Might Be Time for Assisted Living

  • Difficulty with daily activities : bathing, dressing, grooming, or preparing meals consistently.
  • Medication errors or forgetfulness : missing doses or confusion about timing.
  • Unexplained weight loss or poor nutrition : skipping meals or relying on convenience foods.
  • Frequent falls or mobility issues : trouble with stairs, balance, or getting around safely.
  • Changes in personal hygiene or home maintenance : unkempt appearance or neglected household tasks.
  • Increasing isolation or loneliness : withdrawing from friends, family, or hobbies.
  • Worsening chronic conditions : harder to manage diabetes, heart issues, or arthritis at home.
  • Safety concerns : leaving doors unlocked, stove on, or wandering.

If several of these feel familiar, a conversation with your loved one and a visit to a small, caring home can bring clarity and peace of mind.

Facility Tours

What to Ask on a Facility Tour

  1. What is the actual staff-to-resident ratio, especially at night and on weekends?
  2. Are meals prepared fresh and can special dietary needs be fully accommodated?
  3. How are medications managed and who oversees them daily?
  4. What happens if a resident’s care needs increase over time?
  5. Is there a Registered Nurse involved in daily care?
  6. What activities and social opportunities are offered regularly?
  7. How are individualized care plans created and updated with families?
  8. What is truly included in the monthly rate versus extra charges?
  9. How do you handle emergencies and 24/7 coverage?
  10. May we speak with current residents or their family members?

Our Approach

How Our Registered Nurse-Led Team Provides Personalized Care

At Oak & Ember, we intentionally limit our home to just 10 residents with a consistent 2-caregiver ratio. Our care is led by a seasoned Registered Nurse with decades of hospital experience.

  • Every resident receives a truly individualized care plan that evolves with their needs.
  • Health monitoring, medication management and mobility support are clinically attentive.
  • Caregivers build real relationships instead of rushing between large numbers of residents.
  • Families remain closely involved — we see you as part of our care team.

This intimate, nurse-led model is what sets Oak & Ember apart from larger assisted living facilities in Maricopa County.

Important Questions

Other Key Questions to Ask Any Assisted Living Provider

  • How long have the owners and leadership team been working in senior care?
  • What is your staff turnover rate and how do you retain great caregivers?
  • Do you work with long-term care insurance or accept Medicaid (if applicable)?
  • How do you support residents experiencing memory changes or complex medical needs?
  • What does a typical day look like for residents?
  • How do you support end-of-life and hospice care when the time comes?
