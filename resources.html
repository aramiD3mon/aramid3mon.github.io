Resources for Families

Choosing assisted living is a big decision. These free guides are written to help Maricopa County families know what to look for, what to ask, and how our small, RN-led home delivers truly personalized care.

Signs It Might Be Time for Assisted Living

  • Difficulty with daily activities — bathing, dressing, grooming, or preparing meals consistently.
  • Medication errors or forgetfulness — missing doses or confusion about timing.
  • Unexplained weight loss or poor nutrition — skipping meals or relying on convenience foods.
  • Frequent falls or mobility issues — trouble with stairs, balance, or getting around safely.
  • Changes in personal hygiene or home maintenance — unkempt appearance or neglected household tasks.
  • Increasing isolation or loneliness — withdrawing from friends, family, or hobbies.
  • Worsening chronic conditions — harder to manage diabetes, heart issues, arthritis, etc. at home.
  • Safety concerns at home — leaving doors unlocked, stove on, or wandering.

If several of these sound familiar, a conversation with your loved one and a tour of a small, caring home like Oak & Ember can bring peace of mind.

What to Ask on a Facility Tour

  1. What is the staff-to-resident ratio, especially at night?
  2. Are meals chef-prepared and can dietary needs be accommodated?
  3. How are medications managed and who oversees them?
  4. What happens if a resident’s care needs increase?
  5. Is there a Registered Nurse on the team and how involved are they?
  6. What activities and social programs are offered?
  7. How are care plans created and updated with families?
  8. What is included in the monthly rate versus extra fees?
  9. How do you handle emergencies and 24/7 coverage?
  10. May we speak with current residents or their families?

How Our Registered Nurse-Led Team Provides Personalized Care

At Oak & Ember we intentionally stay small — just 10 residents and a consistent 2-caregiver ratio. Our home is led by a Registered Nurse with decades of hospital experience (including charge nurse on a medical-surgical floor). This means:

  • Every resident has a truly individualized care plan updated in real time.
  • Medication, mobility, and health monitoring are handled by clinically experienced staff.
  • Caregivers build genuine relationships instead of rushing between dozens of residents.
  • Families stay closely involved — we treat you as part of the care team.

This intimate, nurse-led model is what sets us apart from larger facilities in Maricopa County.

Other Important Questions to Ask Any Assisted Living Provider

  • How long have the owner and leadership team been in senior care?
  • What is your staff turnover rate and how do you keep great caregivers?
  • Do you accept long-term care insurance or Medicaid (if applicable)?
  • How do you support residents with memory changes or complex medical needs?
  • What does a typical day look like for a resident?
  • How do you handle end-of-life or hospice care if needed?
